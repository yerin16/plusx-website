Pharmacy Campaign: 08.07.2022~10.07.2022

Problem!

Only in Korea, more than 300 tonnes of pharmaceuticals are improperly disposed of into the environment. In addition, recently, chemical compounds of Viagra and painkillers were discovered in the Han River, one of the largest rivers in South Korea. In our current world, many people often do not finish their prescribed medication and dispose of it by casually flushing or simply throwing it away in the trash. However, a significant portion of the public still do not recognize the detrimental consequences of such improper disposal of pharmaceuticals. When medication is inappropriately discarded, the chemical compounds leach into the sewage system. As septic systems are unable to filter the harmful chemicals, the chemicals inevitably infiltrate the marine ecosystem, harming the environment, and our water sources, jeopardizing our own health


Solution

In order for us to raise awareness on this obscure environmental issue and advocate for better disposal methods, our members visited # pharmacies in Gangnam. Upon permission from the store owners, we installed a small pamphlet stand at the pharmacy counter for every visitor to clearly perceive and understand the issue of disposing of medicine at home. In the pamphlet, we mainly encouraged the readers to finish their prescribed medicine. However, the pamphlet also indicated that if the medication cannot be fully completed due to any unfortunate occasions, those medicines should be returned to pharmacies for proper, environmental-friendly disposal. We hoped that the campaign would effectively raise awareness and encourage commitment of the public on the issue of consequences to unsuitable medicine disposal.
